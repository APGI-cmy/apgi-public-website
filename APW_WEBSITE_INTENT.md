## 1. Why this document exists

This document captures the strategic intent, philosophy, and website-direction alignment for the APGI Public Website.

It exists so future corrective stages, builders, reviewers, and assurance passes do not reduce APGI to a conventional services catalogue, a disconnected app directory, or a flat set of product cards.

The APW website must introduce APGI as a loss-prevention and maturity-improvement partner with a holistic operating model supported by consulting, tools, training, monitoring, and assurance.

---

## 2. Core APGI proposition

APGI assists organisations to prevent loss by improving operational maturity.

APGI's approach is based on the principle that losses arise from opportunity, and that organisational environments generate opportunity when governance, process integrity, people, systems, risk management, and controls are weak or immature.

APGI addresses both:

- **malicious loss** - theft, fraud, deliberate wrongdoing, collusion, manipulation, or exploitation of control weaknesses;
- **non-malicious loss** - operational failures, process failures, weak maintenance, poor data, misaligned procedures, variances, weak incentives, human error, or uncontrolled change.

The website must make clear that APGI's work is not limited to guarding, cameras, compliance, audits, or training. APGI helps organisations understand where loss opportunity is created, reduce that opportunity, detect it when it starts to realise, and build resilient operating environments over time.

---

## 3. The maturity journey is the spine of the website

The APW website must present APGI's ecosystem as a maturity journey, not as a disconnected list of apps or services.

The visitor should understand the journey as follows:

1. **Assess current state** - the organisation is assessed against practical criteria and minimum performance expectations.
2. **Understand maturity** - the organisation receives an indication of its current maturity level.
3. **Identify gaps** - weaknesses, missing controls, immature behaviours, and opportunity generators are identified.
4. **Build the roadmap** - a structured maturity roadmap is created to move the organisation toward resilience.
5. **Implement over time** - improvement actions are treated as a long-term project that may run over several years.
6. **Monitor and assure** - controls, work activities, incidents, evidence, and exceptions are monitored and reviewed.
7. **Coach, guide, and train** - APGI supports the organisation through consultation, tools, daily/periodic guidance, and structured training.
8. **Mature toward resilience** - as the organisation matures, manual and human-dependent practices should increasingly become system-supported, automated, evidence-producing, and resilient.

The website must therefore explain why APGI's modules belong together in one maturity ecosystem.

---

## 4. Free maturity assessment entry point

The website should include a clear public entry point for a free maturity assessment.

The purpose of the free assessment is to let an organisation obtain an initial indication of its maturity level and understand where it may sit on the maturity journey.

The free assessment should:

- explain that it is an introductory indication, not a full audit;
- introduce the maturity levels in plain language;
- help the visitor recognise gaps and improvement areas;
- route the visitor toward the ISMS / APGI Hub environment for the deeper assessment journey;
- support a conversion path into consultation, the maturity roadmap, training, and relevant APGI tools.

APW must not reimplement the full assessment engine unless explicitly approved. The public website should introduce the free assessment and hand the user off to the appropriate ISMS/APGI Hub destination when the assessment requires platform functionality.

## 5. Ecosystem roles in the maturity journey

The APGI ecosystem should be explained in relation to the maturity journey.

### Maturity Roadmap / MMM

The maturity assessment and roadmap engine. It helps assess current maturity against criteria, identify gaps, and define the roadmap toward resilient operations.

### Risk Management

The risk logic engine. It connects threats, vulnerabilities, likelihood, consequence, control effectiveness, residual risk, and mitigation planning.

### ISMS / APGI Hub

The platform front door and orchestration layer. It links the maturity journey, module discovery, public-to-platform handoff, assessment entry points, training, and user guidance.

### PIT - Project Implementation Tracking

The implementation tracker for the roadmap. It helps turn the maturity roadmap into a managed programme of actions, accountability, milestones, evidence, and progress over time.

### Incident & Intelligence Hub

The workflow and intelligence layer for incidents, anomalies, loss events, weak signals, operational failures, and investigation or response workflows.

### RADAM / Data Analytics / Remote Assurance

The remote monitoring and independent assurance layer. It uses operational data and system evidence to identify possible loss events, monitor control effectiveness, support daily or periodic assurance, and help resolve issues.

### Skills Development / Training

The capability-building layer. It supports the maturity journey by equipping people with the knowledge and skills needed to improve governance, risk management, process integrity, controls, systems use, and assurance discipline.

## 6. Real-time situational awareness principle

APGI's model includes the principle of real-time or near-real-time situational awareness.

Organisations should increasingly understand:

- what work is being performed;
- who is performing it;
- what risks are associated with the work;
- what controls are required;
- whether those controls are present and effective;
- what evidence is produced;
- what exceptions, anomalies, incidents, or weak signals arise;
- when escalation, coaching, investigation, or mitigation is required.

A mature organisation should be able to identify high-risk activities, high-risk people, missing controls, abnormal work patterns, and possible loss events early enough to intervene.

The APW website should introduce this idea in accessible language without overclaiming live functionality that belongs inside the app ecosystem.

## 7. Hover, click, ask, train learning pattern

APGI's digital experience should support a layered learning model:

1. **Hover** - give a short one-line explanation.
2. **Click** - provide a concise paragraph or modal explanation.
3. **Ask** - invite the user to ask Maturion for deeper contextual guidance.
4. **Train** - direct the user to structured training when the topic requires deeper learning.

The website should use this pattern where appropriate to make complex loss-prevention and maturity concepts understandable without overwhelming the visitor.

Maturion should answer in context and, where useful, refer users to the training platform or relevant learning pathway.

---

## 8. Case study intent and confidentiality rule

The website should eventually include case studies or success stories that show how APGI supports projects, feasibility studies, control design, maturity improvement, remote assurance, and loss-prevention outcomes.

Case studies should follow a safe structure:

- context and challenge;
- risk or opportunity generator;
- APGI involvement;
- control or maturity response;
- operational benefit;
- maturity improvement;
- lessons learned.

Client, project, and company names must be excluded unless explicit permission has been granted. Case studies should be anonymised by default.

---

## 9. Website direction and content obligations

The APW website must explain APGI in a lively, professional, interactive, and credible way.

It should:

- present APGI as a holistic loss-prevention and maturity partner;
- make malicious and non-malicious loss understandable;
- explain how immature environments create opportunity for loss;
- show the maturity journey from current state to resilient operations;
- introduce the free maturity assessment as the public entry point into the deeper journey;
- connect visitors logically to the ISMS/APGI Hub site and relevant applications;
- explain the role of each APGI module in the journey;
- support consultation, training, and platform handoff paths;
- avoid reducing APGI Hub to a flat app-card directory;
- avoid implying that APW itself implements backend platform functions, audits, live monitoring, or LMS administration unless explicitly approved.

---

## 10. Builder and reviewer obligations

Future APW builders and reviewers must treat this file as a root intent anchor.

A build or content correction is not aligned if it:

- presents APGI only as a generic security consultancy;
- presents the APGI ecosystem only as unrelated product cards;
- omits the maturity journey narrative;
- omits the malicious and non-malicious loss-prevention framing;
- omits the assessment-to-roadmap-to-implementation logic;
- fails to explain how tools, training, monitoring, and assurance support maturity progression;
- hides or weakens the public free assessment entry point;
- fails to link the free assessment / deeper maturity journey to the ISMS/APGI Hub destination;
- introduces backend, database, CRM, private API, LMS-admin, or assessment-engine scope into APW without explicit approval.

If a later prebuild artifact, implementation, PR body, or reviewer summary conflicts with this root intent, the conflict must be escalated and resolved through the governed corrective process before implementation is accepted.
