## Purpose

This addendum aligns APW UX Workflow & Wiring with the root website intent canon.

The UX must guide visitors through APGI's loss-prevention maturity journey, not merely present pages, cards, or CTAs.

---

## Required visitor journey

The public UX must support this narrative flow:

1. Visitor understands APGI prevents malicious and non-malicious loss.
2. Visitor understands immature organisational environments create loss opportunity.
3. Visitor sees that APGI assesses current maturity against practical criteria.
4. Visitor is invited to start with a free maturity assessment.
5. Visitor is handed off to ISMS/APGI Hub for the deeper assessment journey where platform functionality is needed.
6. Visitor understands how MMM, Risk Management, PIT, Incident & Intelligence, RADAM, and Training support the roadmap.
7. Visitor can contact APGI for consultation, roadmap support, training, or platform engagement.

---

## Homepage UX obligations

The homepage should introduce the maturity journey early. It should not bury APGI's core proposition below generic services copy.

Required UX elements:

- high-level loss-prevention proposition;
- malicious and non-malicious loss explanation in simple language;
- maturity journey preview;
- free assessment CTA;
- platform/APGI Hub journey handoff;
- training and consultation handoffs.

---

## Platform/APGI Hub UX obligations

The Platform route must be journey-led.

It should explain:

- the problem: opportunity generators create loss;
- the starting point: maturity assessment and criteria;
- the roadmap: actions over time;
- the support system: tools, workflows, monitoring, assurance, coaching, and training;
- the destination: resilient operations.

Module cards may be used, but they must sit inside this journey and not replace it.

---

## Free assessment UX obligations

The free maturity assessment CTA must be visible as a public entry path.

The UX must clearly distinguish:

- APW introductory assessment messaging;
- deeper ISMS/APGI Hub assessment functionality;
- consultation and roadmap follow-up.

APW may link or hand off. It must not pretend to execute the full assessment engine unless that is separately approved.

---

## Hover / click / ask / train interaction model

Where APW introduces complex concepts, the UX should use the layered education pattern:

- **Hover:** one-line explanation.
- **Click:** short detail or modal-style explanation.
- **Ask:** prompt to ask Maturion for context where platform integration exists.
- **Train:** link to structured training where deeper learning is needed.

For APW public pages, this can be implemented as accessible text, cards, expandable panels, modal-style explanations, or clear links. The concept must remain accessible on mobile and keyboard navigation.

---

## Case study UX obligations

Future case-study UX must be anonymised by default and structured around:

- context;
- risk or opportunity generator;
- APGI intervention;
- control/maturity response;
- operational benefit;
- maturity improvement.

No client names or project names may be shown unless explicit permission exists.
